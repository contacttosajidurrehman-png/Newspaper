Kashmir Solidarity Day is a national holiday observed in Pakistan every year on 5 February. This day is dedicated to expressing Pakistan’s unity and support for the people of Indian-administered Jammu and Kashmir. It highlights solidarity with Kashmiris who are struggling for their political rights and pays tribute to those who have lost their lives during the long-standing conflict. On this day, rallies, seminars, human chains, and special prayers are organized across Pakistan. Similar activities are also held in Azad Jammu and Kashmir, as well as by overseas Pakistanis, especially the British Mirpuri community in the United Kingdom. These events aim to raise awareness about the Kashmir issue and draw international attention to the hardships faced by the Kashmiri people. Kashmir Solidarity Day was first proposed in 1990 by Qazi Hussain Ahmad, the former leader of Jamaat-e-Islami Pakistan. The idea was supported. by Nawaz Sharif, who later declared it a national observance. The current formal commemoration structure was introduced in 2004 by the Pakistani Ministry of Kashmir Affairs and Northern Areas. Overall, Kashmir Solidarity Day serves as a reminder of Pakistan’s moral, political, and diplomatic support for the Kashmiri cause. It reinforces the message that Pakistan stands united with the people of Kashmir in their quest for justice, peace, and self-determination.
